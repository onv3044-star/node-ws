Workflow

Four steps from raw data to publication ready figures

A typical Prism workflow includes data entry, statistical selection, graph refinement, and final export.

1

Create a Data Table

Select a suitable table type such as Column, Grouped, XY, or Survival according to the experimental design.

2

Choose an Analysis

Pick the appropriate statistical test or curve fitting model based on grouping, sample size, and data structure.

3

Refine the Graph

4

Export high resolution images or vector files for manuscripts, slides, posters, or further editing.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

This section can be used as a brief webpage introduction and expanded into a tutorial, lab training page, or software guide.

Is GraphPad Prism suitable for beginners?

Yes. Prism has a relatively intuitive interface, and many common statistical and graphing tasks can be completed through guided templates.

Can Prism replace professional statistical software?

Prism is excellent for routine experimental statistics and scientific graphing. For complex modeling or large scale data analysis, tools such as R, Python, SPSS, or SAS may be more appropriate.

What should researchers avoid when making graphs?

Researchers should avoid showing only the mean while hiding raw data points. Error bars, statistical tests, and significance labels should match the experimental design.

Can Prism figures be used in journal submissions?

Yes. Before submission, researchers should check image resolution, font consistency, line thickness, color mode, and the figure format required by the target journal.

Present reliable data with clear scientific visuals

A strong scientific figure should be visually clear, statistically appropriate, and faithful to the original data. GraphPad Prism helps researchers transform experimental results into polished visuals for manuscripts, posters, and presentations.
